    ##### History of Belém
    ---

    Belém was founded by the Portuguese in 1615 and became an important hub for the colonization of the Amazon region and the export of natural resources. During the rubber boom (1879-1912), the city experienced rapid growth and became a cosmopolitan center, attracting immigrants from Europe, Africa, and Asia.

    In recent years, Belém has undergone significant urban development and revitalization efforts, including the restoration of historic buildings and the creation of new cultural and tourist attractions. Today, the city is a thriving cultural and economic center, known for its rich musical and artistic heritage, as well as its unique blend of indigenous, African, and European influences.

    For more reading on Belém, the article about [Belém on Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m) is a great place to learn.

    ##### Geography of Belém
    ---

    Belém is a strategic hub in the Amazon region, serving as the capital of Pará state and the gateway to the Amazon River and its vast basin. Its location at the confluence of the Amazon and Tocantins rivers makes it a crucial transportation hub, connecting the region to the rest of Brazil and the world.

    Geographically, Belém is situated in the eastern part of the Amazon basin, where the Amazon River meets the Guajará Bay, making it a bridge between the rainforest and the Atlantic Ocean. Its proximity to the equator and tropical climate make it an important center for agriculture, with crops like açaí, cocoa, and soybeans driving the local economy.

    ##### Interesting Facts about Belém
    ---

    **Did you know that...** Belém is known as "The City of Mangoes"? This delectable fruit is abundant in the region, and during harvest season, the streets are filled with the sweet aroma of ripe mangoes.

    **Did you know that...** the [Ver-o-Peso Market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ver-o-peso) in Belém is one of the oldest markets in Brazil? Founded in 1625, this market is a vibrant place where you can find a wide variety of fresh products, from exotic fruits to fresh fish from the Amazon.

    **Did you know that...** Belém is the starting point for exploring the Amazon rainforest? From here, you can embark on exciting jungle excursions, where you can see an incredible diversity of flora and fauna, including unique species like the pink dolphin and the jaguar.

    **Did you know that...** the [Círio de Nazaré](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m#C%C3%ADrio_de_Nazar%C3%A9) is one of the largest religious celebrations in Brazil and takes place in Belém? Every year, millions of people participate in this procession in honor of Our Lady of Nazaré, the city's patron saint, in an event that combines religious fervor and cultural tradition.

    **Did you know that...** Belém is known for its delicious Amazonian cuisine? Dishes like ["Tacacá"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tacac%C3%A1) (a hot soup with dried shrimp and jambu), ["Pato no Tucupi"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pato_no_tucupi) (duck cooked in a yellow manioc sauce), and ["Açaí"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A%C3%A7a%C3%AD_na_tigela) (a purple smoothie made with the fruit of the same name) are just some of the culinary delights you can try in the city.

    ##### Arriving by air
    ---

    Belém's Val-de-Cans International Airport is a medium-sized international airport with many direct flights from within Brazil and from other countries.

    The direct international connections are as follows:
    - FLL (Fort Lauderdale, USA) - Azul Airlines
    - LIS (Lisbon, Portugal) - TAP Portugal
    - PBM (Paramaribo, Suriname) - Gol Airlines
    - CAY (Cayenne, French Guyana) - Air France

    ##### Arriving by sea
    ---

    In addition to access by air, Belém has an excellent infrastructure for river transport. El Puerto de Belém connects with several other destinations in the north of Brazil, such as Manaos, Santarém and Macapá.

    To enter Belém do Pará by sea, there are two main entry points:

     - **Puerto de Belém**: Belém has an important commercial port, so arriving by sea directly to this port is a common option.
     - **Puerto de Val de Cans**: this is the port of Belém that receives passenger and tourist boats.

   ##### Neighborhoods of Belém
   ---

Belém, the charming city at the mouth of the Amazon River, has a variety of neighborhoods that offer different experiences for visitors. Here we present a list of the 5 best neighborhoods to stay, according to amenities, accessibility and attractions:

- **Batista Campos**: Located in the center of the city, it is known for its tree-lined streets and peaceful atmosphere.
- **Campina**: Known for its colonial architecture and traditional atmosphere, it is ideal for those looking to immerse themselves in local culture.
- **Marco**: This neighborhood offers a panoramic view of the Guamá River and is famous for its craft market.
- **Cidade Velha**: It is the oldest neighborhood in Belém, with cobblestone streets, beautiful colonial churches, and lots of historic charm.
- **Umarizal**: It is the city's financial district, with a wide range of accommodation options and a vibrant nightlife.
