  <div>
    ##### History of Belém
    ---

    Belém was founded by the Portuguese in 1615 and became an important hub for the colonization of the Amazon region and the export of natural resources. During the rubber boom (1879-1912), the city experienced rapid growth and became a cosmopolitan center, attracting immigrants from Europe, Africa, and Asia.

    In recent years, Belém has undergone significant urban development and revitalization efforts, including the restoration of historic buildings and the creation of new cultural and tourist attractions. Today, the city is a thriving cultural and economic center, known for its rich musical and artistic heritage, as well as its unique blend of indigenous, African, and European influences.

    For more reading on Belém, the article about [Belém on Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m) is a great place to learn.

  </div>
  <div>
  ##### Geography of Belém
    ---

    Belém is a strategic hub in the Amazon region, serving as the capital of Pará state and the gateway to the Amazon River and its vast basin. Its location at the confluence of the Amazon and Tocantins rivers makes it a crucial transportation hub, connecting the region to the rest of Brazil and the world.

    Geographically, Belém is situated in the eastern part of the Amazon basin, where the Amazon River meets the Guajará Bay, making it a bridge between the rainforest and the Atlantic Ocean. Its proximity to the equator and tropical climate make it an important center for agriculture, with crops like açaí, cocoa, and soybeans driving the local economy.

  </div>

    ---

    Belém's Val-de-Cans International Airport is a medium-sized international airport with many direct flights from within Brazil and from other countries.

    ##### Arriving by sea
    ---

    In addition to access by air, Belém has an excellent infrastructure for river transport. El Puerto de Belém connects with several other destinations in the north of Brazil, such as Manaos, Santarém and Macapá.

    To enter Belém do Pará by sea, there are two main entry points:

     - **Puerto de Belém**: Belém has an important commercial port, so arriving by sea directly to this port is a common option.
     - **Puerto de Val de Cans**: this is the port of Belém that receives passenger and tourist boats.

   ##### Neighborhoods of Belém

Belém, the charming city at the mouth of the Amazon River, has a variety of neighborhoods that offer different experiences for visitors. Here we present a list of the 5 best neighborhoods to stay, according to amenities, accessibility and attractions:
